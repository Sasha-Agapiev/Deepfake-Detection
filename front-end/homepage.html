About DDS

The days of newspapers and tabloids are long gone.

Nowadays, the great majority of people get their news and information from online sources, but unfortunately not all of this information is authentic. Recent technological breakthroughs have made it easier for misinformation to spread online with no way for internet users to protect themselves.

As college students who lived through the age of online classes and the Zoom University experience, we are all too aware of this trend. This is why we decided to create the Deepfake Detection System. By simply creating an account our Chrome extension platform, we give you the ability to verify the authenticity of any kind of digital image in a matter of seconds. Our backend technology uses the latest machine learning strategies to calculate the likelihood of a picture being a deepfake, so that you can continue to browse in peace without the looming fear of getting misled.

To use our system, simply create or sign-in to your account with your email. Then, once directed to the user page, upload any .png or .jpg image using the "Upload Image" button. Then, wait a few seconds to get a prediction of whether or not this image is a deepfake. If you find a website that is sharing deepfake content and would like to report it to our database, click the "Report Website" button.

Deepfake Detection System designed and implemented by Team B25 from NYU Tandon School of Engineering
